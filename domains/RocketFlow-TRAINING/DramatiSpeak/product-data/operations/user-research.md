# User Research & Insights: DramatiSpeak™

**Last Updated:** December 19, 2025

---

## Research Overview

### Pilot Study (Nov 4 - Dec 15, 2025)

**Methodology:**
* 42 pilot households across 3 cities (NYC, Austin, Portland)
* Mixed methods: quantitative usage data + qualitative interviews
* Weekly surveys + end-of-pilot in-depth interviews
* Species breakdown: 28 cats, 14 dogs

**Research Goals:**
1. Validate core value proposition (drama translation entertains and informs)
2. Test ML accuracy perception and believability
3. Identify friction points in setup and daily use
4. Understand sharing behavior and viral potential
5. Explore feature requests and enhancement opportunities

---

## Key Findings

### Finding #1: Product Delivers on Entertainment Promise ✅

**Evidence:**
* 94% of users report device "made them laugh out loud" within first week
* 78% shared at least one dramatic translation on social media
* Average 3.4 social shares per user per week
* NPS score of 64 (above target of 55)

**User Quotes:**

> "I literally screamed laughing when DramatiSpeak told me my cat was 'performing theatrical disappointment' because I chose the wrong brand of treats. Drama Index: 89. She's so dramatic!" - Emma, 28, NYC (Cat owner)

> "My husband didn't believe me when I said our dog was faking hunger. Now I have proof. Drama Index: 91 for 'fake hunger alert' 20 minutes after breakfast. Vindication!" - Jordan, 34, Austin (Dog owner)

> "I check the app every morning like it's my horoscope. What will Mittens be judging me for today?" - Alyssa, 29, Portland (Cat owner)

**Insight:** Core value prop is validated. Users find daily entertainment value, not just novelty.

---

### Finding #2: Accuracy Perception is Critical (and Currently Mixed) 🟡

**Evidence:**
* Overall ML accuracy: 78% (user-validated thumbs up/down)
* 67% of users say translations are "usually accurate"
* 23% say "sometimes accurate"
* 10% say "rarely accurate"

**Accuracy Varies by Drama Category:**
* High Accuracy (85%+): Judgmental Cat Mode, Attention Deficit Theater
* Moderate Accuracy (70-80%): Fake Hunger Alert, Existential Crisis
* Low Accuracy (60-70%): Dramatic Sighing, Seasonal Judgment

**User Quotes:**

> "When it gets it right, it's REALLY right. Like scary right. But when it's wrong, it breaks the magic. I need to trust it to enjoy it." - Michael, 31, NYC (Cat owner)

> "Judgmental Cat Mode is so accurate it's creepy. But 'dramatic sighing'? My dog was actually just tired. That one needs work." - Taylor, 26, Austin (Dog owner)

> "I don't need it to be 100% accurate—that would be weird. But it needs to be believable. Like 8 out of 10 times I should be nodding my head." - Sarah, 36, Portland (Family, Dog owner)

**Insight:** 75-80% accuracy is acceptable, but users need confidence in the system. Improving low-performing categories is critical to maintaining trust.

---

### Finding #3: Multi-Pet Households Show Highest Engagement 📈

**Evidence:**
* 4 pilot households had 2 pets (1 had both cat and dog)
* Multi-pet households averaged 5.7 DAT (vs. 3.2 single-pet)
* Multi-pet users requested "drama comparison" features most frequently
* Retention: 100% of multi-pet households still active at Week 6 (vs. 69% overall)

**User Quotes:**

> "I need to see which pet is more dramatic. My cat acts like she's the drama queen, but I suspect my dog is secretly winning." - Lisa, 32, NYC (Cat + Dog owner)

> "My two cats have completely different personalities. Being able to compare their Drama Index scores is hilarious. Cleo is always at 80+, while Oliver is a chill 30." - David, 29, Austin (2 Cats)

**Insight:** Multi-pet support should be prioritized. Creates competition dynamic and significantly boosts engagement.

---

### Finding #4: Sharing Behavior is Social Currency 🔥

**Evidence:**
* 78% of pilot users shared translations on social media
* Average 3.4 shares per user per week
* Most shared content: High Drama Index moments (85+)
* Sharing peaked during "relatable moments" (e.g., fake hunger, judgment)

**What Users Share:**
* Screenshots of translations with Drama Index scores
* "My pet is more dramatic than yours" comparisons
* Weekly drama reports (requested feature)
* Funny/unexpected translations

**User Quotes:**

> "I send every translation to my family group chat. It's our new entertainment. My mom now asks daily, 'What did Buddy say today?'" - Jordan, 34, Austin

> "I posted a screenshot of my cat's 'Judgmental Cat Mode' translation and it got 450 likes on Instagram. My highest performing post ever." - Emma, 28, NYC

> "My friends started calling my dog 'Drama King' because his average Drama Index is 76. It's become his identity." - Chris, 27, Portland

**Insight:** Product is inherently shareable. Users derive social value from sharing dramatic pet moments. Need to optimize sharing UX and create "weekly drama report" graphics.

---

### Finding #5: Notification Strategy Needs Refinement 📲

**Evidence:**
* Notification open rate declined from 71% (Week 1) to 52% (Week 6)
* Users report "too many notifications" during peak drama times
* 38% of users turned off notifications and check app manually
* "Do Not Disturb" hours feature requested by 62% of users

**User Feedback:**

> "I love the translations, but 7 notifications before 9am is too much. My cat is most dramatic in the morning, but I can't check my phone constantly." - Rachel, 30, NYC

> "I wish I could set 'quiet hours' so I only get notifications when I'm actually home and able to engage." - Mark, 35, Austin

> "The notifications lose their magic when there are too many. I want to be surprised, not spammed." - Alyssa, 29, Portland

**Insight:** Need intelligent notification batching, "Do Not Disturb" scheduling, and frequency controls. Consider daily digest option for high-drama pets.

---

### Finding #6: Device Comfort is a Barrier for Some Pets 🐾

**Evidence:**
* 79% of pets wore device comfortably
* 14% of pets refused to wear device (primarily cats)
* 7% tolerated device with visible resistance

**Churn Analysis:**
* 44% of pilot dropouts cited "pet won't tolerate collar" as primary reason

**User Quotes:**

> "My cat Mochi absolutely refused to wear the collar. She went into protest mode—hiding, refusing food, the whole drama. Ironically, the device that detects drama caused drama." - Jen, 27, NYC (Dropped out Week 2)

> "The collar is too bulky for my small cat. She kept trying to scratch it off. I felt bad and stopped using it." - Alex, 31, Portland (Dropped out Week 3)

> "My dog didn't even notice the collar. Wish all pets were this easy." - Jordan, 34, Austin (Still active)

**Insight:** Need lighter, sleeker collar design, especially for cats. Consider clip-on version for harness users. Pet acceptance is critical to retention.

---

### Finding #7: Users Want Deeper Insights Over Time 📊

**Evidence:**
* 83% of users requested "Drama History" timeline feature
* 71% interested in "drama pattern insights" (e.g., "Your cat is most judgmental on Monday mornings")
* 56% want to export data for trainers/vets
* 48% interested in "drama predictions" based on historical patterns

**Feature Requests (by frequency):**
1. **Drama History timeline** (35 requests) - See past dramatic moments organized by date
2. **Multi-pet comparison** (28 requests) - Side-by-side drama charts
3. **Weekly drama report** (22 requests) - Automated summary with fun graphics
4. **Do Not Disturb hours** (21 requests) - Schedule quiet times
5. **Custom drama categories** (18 requests) - Train AI on personal pet quirks
6. **Pattern insights** (16 requests) - "Your dog is dramatic every Tuesday at 3pm"
7. **Drama leaderboard** (14 requests) - Compare with friends' pets
8. **Voice-enabled translations** (12 requests) - Hear translations via Alexa/Google
9. **Vet notes export** (9 requests) - Share behavioral data with vet
10. **Prediction mode** (8 requests) - "Drama likely in next 2 hours based on patterns"

**User Quotes:**

> "After 6 weeks, I want to see long-term patterns. Is my cat MORE dramatic on certain days? Does weather affect her mood? I want the data." - Michael, 31, NYC

> "A weekly summary card I could share on Instagram would be perfect. Like Spotify Wrapped but for pet drama." - Taylor, 26, Austin

> "I'd pay extra for 'drama predictions' so I know when to expect the fake hunger performance." - Jordan, 34, Austin

**Insight:** Users want to graduate from "entertaining moment" to "behavioral insight platform." Consider tiered features: free (real-time), paid (historical analysis + predictions).

---

### Finding #8: Trust is Built Through Transparency 🔍

**Evidence:**
* Users who understood "how it works" had 23% higher retention
* 62% wanted to see "confidence scores" for each translation
* 54% interested in "why the AI made this determination"

**User Quotes:**

> "I want to know WHY DramatiSpeak thinks my cat is judging me. Show me the body language signals or something so I can learn too." - Emma, 28, NYC

> "If the translation says 'Low confidence: 60%' I'd be more forgiving when it's wrong. Just be honest with me." - Sarah, 36, Portland

> "I'm a data person. Give me the details: What signals triggered this? What's the accuracy rate for this category? I'll trust it more if you show your work." - Jordan, 34, Austin

**Insight:** Transparency features build trust. Consider adding "confidence score," "Why this translation?" explainer, and per-category accuracy stats in settings.

## Behavioral Insights

### Usage Patterns

**Peak Usage Times:**
* **Morning (6-9am):** 38% of translations - Pets most dramatic during breakfast/departure
* **Evening (5-8pm):** 31% of translations - Return home + dinner time drama
* **Midday (12-2pm):** 18% of translations - Lunchtime attention-seeking
* **Night (8pm-12am):** 13% of translations - Pre-bed routines

**Day of Week Patterns:**
* Monday: 17% above average drama (return to work guilt-tripping)
* Friday: 12% above average drama (anticipation of weekend attention)
* Sunday evening: 19% above average drama (pre-workweek anxiety)

## Competitive Intelligence from Users

### What Users Compared DramatiSpeak To:

1. **FitBark/Whistle** (mentioned by 62% of users)
   * "Way more fun than a health tracker"
   * "I actually check DramatiSpeak every day, vs. FitBark once a month"
   * "Health trackers are boring. This is entertainment."

2. **MeowTalk** (mentioned by 38% of users)
   * "MeowTalk was cute for 5 minutes. This actually works."
   * "App-only doesn't capture enough context. Hardware makes a difference."
   * "MeowTalk felt like a toy. DramatiSpeak feels like a real product."

3. **Furbo/Petcube** (mentioned by 29% of users)
   * "I love Furbo, but it's passive. DramatiSpeak is proactive."
   * "Cameras are for when I'm away. DramatiSpeak is for when I'm home."
   * "Furbo doesn't tell me what my pet is thinking. Just shows me."

**Key Takeaway:** Users position DramatiSpeak as "entertainment + insight," not health/safety. This is our differentiated space.

## Feature Prioritization (Based on User Research)

### Must-Have for Launch
1. ✅ Real-time drama translation (core feature)
2. ✅ Drama Index scoring (0-100)
3. ✅ Push notifications
4. ✅ Social sharing (screenshot format)
5. 🔧 Multi-pet support (high engagement multiplier)

1. Drama History timeline
2. Weekly drama report cards
3. Pattern insights ("Your cat is most dramatic on Mondays")
4. Confidence scores for translations
5. Custom drama categories (user-trained)

### Medium Priority (Q3 2026)
1. Drama leaderboard (compare with friends)
2. Voice-enabled translations (Alexa/Google integration)
3. Vet notes export

### Low Priority / Future Exploration
1. Drama predictions
2. Pet-to-pet communication (two pets "talking" to each other)

## Recommendations

### Immediate Actions (Pre-Launch)
1. **Redesign collar** - 21% pet rejection rate too high
2. **Improve "Dramatic Sighing" model** - Accuracy of 64% undermines trust
3. **Add Do Not Disturb feature** - Notification fatigue is real
4. **Optimize battery to 7+ days** - Current 5.2 days below expectation

### Launch Strategy
1. **Target Alyssa persona heavily** - Highest engagement, best fit, strongest advocacy
2. **Seed 100+ pet influencers** - Proven viral potential (780K impressions from 4 users)
3. **Lead with entertainment positioning** - "Drama translation," not "pet tracker"
4. **Show accuracy data prominently** - Transparency builds trust

### Post-Launch Priorities (Q2-Q3 2026)
1. **Multi-pet support** - 2.3x engagement boost
2. **Drama History timeline** - Most requested feature
3. **Weekly drama reports** - Shareable, drives virality
4. **Pattern insights** - Moves product from novelty to utility
